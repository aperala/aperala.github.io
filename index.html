About

Why would someone with a B.A. in English and experience in teaching, publishing, and bookselling want to be a web developer?

Good question.

Well, I've always liked project-based work. I've managed projects of all sizes, including conferences, trade show exhibits, and print catalog production. Once, I led a team in constructing a seven-foot-long dragon out of wire and fabric.

As an English grammar and writing teacher, I'm fascinated by the syntax and structure of languages. And my experience working with inventory systems and linguistic corpora informs my interest in data and data visualization.

What is a person who needs to be learning new things all the time, who likes to create things—but who also likes to solve puzzles and who is really finicky about things like the proper use of semicolons—supposed to do?

Make things for the web.

Sure, sometimes I hit a snag that makes me want to bang my head against the wall. But most of the time the work is so absorbing that it doesn't feel like work at all.

When not in front of my laptop, I will most likely be found reading, exploring New York with my dog, or playing tennis.
