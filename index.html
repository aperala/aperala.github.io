About

Why would someone with a B.A. in English and experience in teaching, publishing, and bookselling want to be a web developer?

Good question.

I like project-based work. I've managed projects of all sizes, including conferences, trade show exhibits, and print catalog production. Once, I led a team in constructing a seven-foot-long dragon out of wire and fabric.

As a former English language teacher, I'm fascinated by the syntax and structure of languages.

What's a person who needs to learn new things and wants to create useful things—but who also likes puzzles and is really finicky about stuff like the proper use of semicolons—supposed to do?

Make things for the web.

What good does this do you? I bring to my work:

✓ A marketing director's ability to meet deadlines
✓ A language teacher's communication skills
✓ A grammar nerd's eye for detail and accuracy
✓ A factotum's willingness to pitch in wherever needed without attitude

If you need some copy written for your website, I can help with that, too.

When not in front of my laptop, I will most likely be found reading, exploring New York with my dog, or playing tennis.
